Research Overview

Exploring layered nanomaterials and advanced inorganic chemistry.

Research in the Salguero laboratory bridges inorganic synthesis, nanotechnology, and materials science. We explore new synthetic methods, characterize structural and chemical transitions at the atomic level, and assemble nanostructured components into functional configurations. Our active research is focused on three main thrust areas:

1. Inorganic Synthesis

We explore solid-state and solution-phase reactions to synthesize new complex metal oxides, chalcogenides, and silicates. By controlling crystal structure and stoichiometry, we tune physical characteristics like ionic conductivity, magnetic interactions, and optical bandgaps.

2. 2D Nanosheet Chemistry

Using liquid-phase exfoliation strategies, we dissect layered crystals down to single-atom or single-octahedron sheet thicknesses. We study the surface chemistry, colloid stability, and chemical functionalization parameters of these 2D sheets in suspension.

3. Layered Assemblies

We develop film assembly protocols (like layer-by-layer coating and spin deposition) to stack distinct nanosheet varieties into custom superlattice configurations. These artificial materials are optimized for solid-state micro-batteries and catalysts.
