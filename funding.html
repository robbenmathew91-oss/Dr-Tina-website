Sponsorship Impact

🏆 Scientific Discoveries Enabled

Sponsorship from NSF and DOE BES has directly enabled discoveries in transition metal dichalcogenide CDW sliding mechanisms, 1D atomic chains isolation, and the exfoliation chemistry of ancient pigments.

🎓 Graduate & Student Training

Grants support student fellowships (including GEM Fellows) and funding for Ph.D. and M.S. graduate theses, producing highly skilled scientists now working in academia, national labs, and industrial microelectronics.

🔬 Shared Collaborations

Our funding models actively finance instrumentation time at national microscopy centers (Oak Ridge National Lab) and microfabrication facilities (Georgia Tech cleanrooms).

🔌 Technological & Societal Relevance

By researching replacement candidates for standard copper interconnects and silicon semiconductors, our sponsors fund research tackling the energy consumption limits of global computational infrastructure.
