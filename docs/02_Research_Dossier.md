# Research Dossier: Salguero Lab

This document outlines the core scientific areas, active projects, lab methodologies, equipment, and safety guidelines for Dr. Tina Salguero's research group.

---

## 1. Primary Research Areas
Dr. Salguero's group focuses on materials chemistry, specializing in inorganic synthesis, nanosheets, and advanced materials.

### Area 1: Inorganic Materials Synthesis
- Focus on the synthesis of novel inorganic compounds with specific electronic, optical, or magnetic properties.
- Exploratory synthesis techniques, solid-state reactions, and hydrothermal methods.

### Area 2: Nanosheet Exfoliation and Chemistry
- Exfoliation of layered materials into single-layer nanosheets.
- Surface modification and functionalization of nanosheets for device integration.
- Layer-by-layer assembly of hybrid nanostructures.

### Area 3: Nanomaterials for Energy and Catalysis
- Developing nanostructured catalysts for clean energy applications.
- Investigating charge-transport mechanisms in novel 2D materials.

---

## 2. Experimental Methodologies & Characterization
The group utilizes a suite of characterization techniques to analyze synthesised materials:
- **X-ray Diffraction (XRD):** Powder and single-crystal XRD for crystal structure determination.
- **Microscopy (SEM, TEM, AFM):** Surface morphology, thickness measurements of nanosheets, and elemental mapping.
- **Spectroscopy (UV-Vis, Raman, FT-IR):** Optical and chemical characterization of materials.
- **Thermal Analysis (TGA, DSC):** Phase transitions and thermal stability measurements.

## 4. Lab Safety & Operations
All researchers must adhere to University of Georgia (UGA) Environmental Safety Division (ESD) standards.
- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Safety glasses, lab coats, and appropriate gloves are mandatory at all times in the wet lab area.
- **Chemical Hygiene Plan:** Review Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before handling new reagents. Store waste in designated containers.
- **Emergency Protocols:** Familiarize yourself with the location of eye wash stations, safety showers, fire extinguishers, and spill kits.
